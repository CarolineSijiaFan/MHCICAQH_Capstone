Smart Attestation

Reimagining provider attestations through distributed micro-attestations

Final Solutions / Smart Attestation

The Problem

Attestations are long forms that providers are required to review every 90 days - burdensome, time-consuming, and seen by providers and proxies as a barrier to providing care.

Current State

Currently, providers must go into CAQH's Provider Data Portal to attest to many provider data fields, including their practice location, licenses and insurance, provider information (NPI), etc. so that their data can be maintained in insurance plans' directories so patients can find them.

Research Question

How might we turn attestation from a compliance task into a moment of mutual value?

We wanted to explore how we could bring value to the attestation experience for providers‚Äîboth by making it easier and by tying it to things they care about.

Our Solution

Instead of periodic, manual attestations, we proposed a new distributed model called "micro-attestations", where providers and proxies verify or update their provider information directly in the tools they already use.

How It Works

While users are in their existing tools, they have the option to autofill with CAQH. When they log into their CAQH account to autofill, they're simultaneously validating that information, and when they make changes, those are also automatically pushed back to CAQH for approval. This creates a seamless data loop between CAQH and everyday workflows.
