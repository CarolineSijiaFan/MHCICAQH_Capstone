Reimagining Provider Attestations through Distributed Moments of Value

Final Solutions / Smarter Attestations

The Problem

Provider attestations are essential but often viewed as burdensome. Every 90 days, providers must review lengthy forms to update various data fields‚Äîsuch as their practice location, licenses, insurance, and provider information (e.g., NPI)‚Äîwithin CAQH's Provider Data Portal.

This process is time-consuming and, for many providers and proxies (e.g., practice managers), a significant barrier to care. Currently, providers must complete attestations so that their information remains accurate and visible in insurance plan directories, enabling patients to find their healthcare providers.

We set out to answer the question: How might we transform attestation from a compliance task into a moment of mutual value for providers and the entire healthcare ecosystem?

Our Solution

Instead of periodic, manual attestations, we propose a new, distributed model called micro-attestations. This allows providers and proxies to verify or update their provider information directly within the tools they already use, seamlessly integrating attestations into their everyday workflows.

Micro-Attestations in Action

Here is what we think this process can look like in the wild.

Imagine you're a provider ordering new prescription pads. Normally, you would have to enter a lot of information‚Äîyour National Provider Number (NPI), practice location, DEA license, and other details to verify your identity.

With CAQH autofill, there's an embedded button on the website (e.g., RxMinutemanPress). Clicking it opens a panel that securely connects to your CAQH data, allowing you to log in and grant just the right amount of information to complete the form.

Login demonstration

Once logged in, you select your practice location and hit autofill, instantly populating the form with your details.

Autofill demonstration

After reviewing, if any details (e.g., practice address or phone number) have changed, you can update them directly in the form. The system detects the changes and asks if you'd like to update your CAQH profile.

Update demonstration

Macro-Attestation: The Final Review

You can then review the updates either right away or on your own time in the CAQH Provider Data Portal. Instead of reviewing every individual field like traditional attestations, you'll see a full paper trail‚Äîtracking what updates were made, where, and when.

Macro-review demonstration

You'll be able to quickly approve or reject updates, then attest immediately, knowing your information is fresh and accurate, without needing to manually check every field.

Looking Ahead

Here is our proposed implementation roadmap:

Next

Establish commercial partnerships and launch embedded micro-attestation flows in third-party platforms.

Later

Position CAQH as a secure OAuth provider to enable seamless, trusted data sharing across the ecosystem.

A Note on OAuth

The future of data sharing in healthcare

By becoming an OAuth provider, CAQH has the potential to transform the healthcare industry. Providers could log into websites across the healthcare ecosystem (e.g., Zocdoc, UpToDate, Epic, Walgreens, UPMC) using their CAQH account.

This would enable automatic and secure data exchange for everything related to provider information, streamlining workflows and potentially eliminating the need for some forms entirely.

In this vision, CAQH becomes the identity layer for the healthcare workforce, serving as the trusted source of truth for administrative data, enhancing efficiency across the healthcare ecosystem.

This would position CAQH as indispensable, empowering health plans, providers, and the broader healthcare system with accurate, real-time data.
