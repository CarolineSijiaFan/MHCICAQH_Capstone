Our Process

Our approach to envisioning the future of CAQH

We started with the problem of unlocking new use cases for CAQH's provider data platform.

Our initial focus was on exploring how CAQH could expand beyond traditional credentialing and directory management to enable new applications and services that would benefit providers, health plans, and ultimately patients.

Research Phase

Spring Semester (January - April 2025)

We conducted 20+ stakeholder interviews to understand the problem space.

Stakeholder included users of CAQH data, subject matter experts, internal CAQH team members, and industry professionals. We then synthesized findings from interviews to uncover patterns, pain points, and opportunities across the healthcare data ecosystem.

Other Research Methods

In addition to interviews, we also conducted:

  • Data flow modeling
  • Persona mapping
  • Analogous domain research

Problem Reframing

Instead of just focusing on unlocking new use cases, we decided to also include data quality and source as an area to explore.

From our research, we expanded beyond just new use cases to identify three critical areas to explore during the summer:

  • Improving data quality through intelligent attestation moments
  • Laying a clear path for more plans to save using CAQH's PSV
  • Supporting easier data analysis across the technical spectrum

Design Phase

Summer Semester (May - July 2025)

Generative Design

Team ideation session

Ideation Phase

Came up with a lot of ideas using crazy 8s methodology to rapidly generate diverse solution concepts across our three identified problem areas.

Decision Point

We were at a crossroads at the end of parallel prototyping - should we do all of them? Pick one? We needed a systematic approach to prioritize.

Lean UX Hypotheses

Developed lean UX hypotheses to help us prioritize and validate our solution concepts with stakeholders and users.

Prioritization Matrix

Created a systematic prioritization framework to evaluate each solution concept based on impact, feasibility, and strategic alignment.

Strategic Decision

We decided to pursue all three solutions as they were all high leverage opportunities. Initially planned to potentially focus on just one by the end of the phase.

We tested 11 prototypes, including

  • Storyboards for assumption testing
  • Card sorts for prioritization
  • Interactive Figma mockups and wireframes
  • Concept demos and proof-of-concepts
  • Even conducted guerrilla research - talking to a provider during a team member's doctor appointment!

Tested with various stakeholders including:

  • Healthcare providers and practice managers
  • Data analysts and credentialing representatives
  • Directory management professionals
  • High-level executives and decision makers

At the end of the generative designphase, we validated user desirabiltiy for all three solutions, and continued to iterate on the form of our solutions.

Evaluative Design

Conducted comprehensive iterative testing for all three solution concepts, continuously refining based on stakeholder feedback and user insights.

13 Prototypes Created

Created 13 more prototypes including Figma prototypes, functional prototypes, card sorts, and interactive demos.

Comprehensive Testing

Tested with the same diverse stakeholder groups: providers, practice managers, data analysts, credentialing reps, and directory management professionals.

Continuous Iteration

Each testing cycle informed the next iteration, leading to increasingly refined and validated solutions.

Final Prototyping

End-to-End Solutions

Created refined, functional prototypes for all three solutions that addressed end-to-end problems and demonstrated real value to stakeholders.

Smart Attestation

Developed working prototypes showing micro-attestations in action, including embeded CAQH Autofill and Micro-attestation, a browser extension, and Provider Data Portal redesigned.

Primary Source Verification

Created comprehensive PSV workflow demonstrations showing the upstream verification process and potential cost savings.

Data Analysis & Retrieval

Built functional prototypes integrating LLM capabilities into the CAQH portal for enhanced data analysis.
